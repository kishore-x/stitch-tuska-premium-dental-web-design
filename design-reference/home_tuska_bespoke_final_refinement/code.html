FAQ

Frequently Asked Questions

1. What age group do you treat? add

We provide comprehensive dental care for children from infancy through adolescence and young adulthood, up to 20 years of age.

2. When should my child have their first dental visit? add

Ideally, your child's first dental visit should be by their first birthday or within six months after the first tooth erupts. Early visits help establish healthy habits and identify concerns at an early stage.

3. My child is scared of dentists. Can you help? add

Yes. Every child is different, and we adapt the visit according to your child's age, comfort level, previous experiences, and individual needs. Our aim is to build trust gradually and create a positive relationship with dental care.

4. Do you treat children with special healthcare needs? add

Yes. We provide individualized care based on each child's medical, developmental, sensory, behavioural, and emotional needs.

5. Do you provide emergency dental care for children? add

Yes. We assess and manage dental injuries, toothache, swelling, broken teeth, and other urgent dental concerns in children.

6. Do you provide preventive dental care? add

Yes. Preventive care may include routine dental check-ups, professional cleaning, fluoride therapy, sealants, dietary guidance, oral hygiene education, and personalized preventive plans.

7. Do baby teeth really need treatment if they are going to fall out anyway? add

Yes. Baby teeth play an important role in eating, speech, jaw development, maintaining space, and guiding the permanent teeth. Treatment is recommended whenever necessary to protect your child's comfort and oral health.

8. Do you assess jaw growth, developing teeth, and oral habits? add

Yes. We assess your child's developing teeth, jaws, bite, oral habits, and growth-related concerns. When necessary, we may also recommend appropriate treatment or multidisciplinary referral.

9. Can I stay with my child during dental treatment? add

Yes. Parents are welcome to be with their child. However, every child is different, and some children may feel more comfortable and cooperative with a parent nearby, while others may respond better with a little independence. The approach will be guided by your child's individual needs and by whichever environment creates the most comfortable and positive dental experience for them. Parents will not be routinely prevented from accompanying their child.

10. My child snores at night, has bad breath, and experiences disturbed sleep. Can a visit with you help us understand what to do next? add

Yes. Snoring, mouth breathing, disturbed sleep, and persistent bad breath can have several possible causes. A detailed dental and oral assessment may help identify contributing factors involving the mouth, jaws, oral habits, or airway-related development. When required, we can guide you towards further evaluation with the appropriate specialists.

11. My child has an adenoid problem and also seems to have improper jaw growth. Can you help? add

Yes. Adenoid-related breathing concerns and jaw development can sometimes be interconnected. We can assess your child's oral structures, bite, jaw growth, and breathing-related signs and, when necessary, work alongside or refer to ENT specialists and other relevant healthcare professionals for coordinated care.

12. I am a breastfeeding mother and continue to experience pain even after latch correction. Can you assess my baby? add

Yes. Persistent breastfeeding discomfort can have multiple causes. We can assess your baby's oral structures, tongue movement, jaw development, and other oral factors that may influence feeding. Based on the findings, we can provide guidance and recommend further evaluation with appropriate professionals, such as a lactation consultant, pediatrician, ENT specialist, or other relevant specialists when needed.

13. My child always breathes through the mouth. Should I be concerned? add

Persistent mouth breathing is worth evaluating. We can assess your child's oral and facial development, jaw growth, bite, and other related signs. If an airway or ENT-related concern is suspected, we can guide you towards the appropriate specialist evaluation.

14. My child grinds their teeth while sleeping. Is this normal? add

Teeth grinding can occur in children for different reasons. During an evaluation, we can check for tooth wear, bite-related factors, oral habits, and other associated signs and advise whether observation, dental management, or further evaluation is appropriate.

15. My child is a picky eater or has difficulty chewing certain foods. Can a dental assessment help? add

Yes. Difficulty chewing or avoiding certain food textures can sometimes be associated with dental problems, oral discomfort, bite, jaw function, or oral-motor concerns. We can assess the dental and oral factors and guide you towards additional professional support when needed.

16. My child has a thumb-sucking, finger-sucking, or pacifier habit. When should I seek help? add

It is helpful to assess persistent oral habits early, particularly when they begin to affect the developing teeth, bite, or jaw growth. We can evaluate the habit and its effects and suggest an age-appropriate approach to help your child.

17. My child had a tongue-tie or lip-tie diagnosis. Do you evaluate these conditions? add

Yes. We can assess the child's oral structures and function in the context of feeding, speech, oral hygiene, and dental development. Treatment decisions are made based on functional needs rather than appearance alone, and multidisciplinary evaluation may be recommended where appropriate.

18. My child's permanent teeth are coming in crooked. Should we wait until all the permanent teeth erupt? add

No. Some developing bite and jaw concerns may benefit from earlier assessment. An evaluation can help determine whether your child simply needs monitoring or whether early intervention may be beneficial.

19. How can I book an appointment? add

Appointments can be booked by contacting Tuskaè through our available phone, WhatsApp, or online appointment options.

20. Do you offer guidance on maintaining oral hygiene at home for children? add

Yes. We provide personalized guidance on brushing techniques, flossing, diet, and daily oral care routines tailored to your child's age and needs to help maintain optimal oral health at home.
